I am based in Linz, Austria, and currently work at Software Competence Center Hagenberg (SCCH). My background is in mathematics, physics, and statistics, and my doctoral work, carried out within the S3AI project, focused on invariant geometric structures, notably convexity interpreted as space folding, under the mapping of sets from continuous input space to the discrete activation space of neural networks. I work across machine learning, neural-network geometry, mechanistic interpretability, and surrogate models for physical simulation, with additional experience in computer vision, natural language processing, and time-series modeling.

Neural-network geometry and folding

Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence (AAAI), 2026

Exploiting Space Folding by Neural Networks

In this paper we prove several properties of the space folding measure $\chi$ along a path $\Gamma$, extend its construction to any activation function, and link it to adversarial geometry under PGD and FGSM attacks.

Paper
Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR), 2025

On Space Folds of ReLU Neural Networks

In this paper we ask which structures are preserved when a ReLU network maps continuous input space to its discrete activation-pattern space. Focusing on convexity, we show that networks with more than one hidden layer need not preserve it, and introduce the space folding measure $\chi$ to quantify the resulting deviations. The measure is sensitive to Kolmogorov-style compactness of neural-network representations, suggesting a role in neural architecture search, and correlates strongly with depth among models with low generalization error.

Accepted at multiple International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) 2026 workshops

A Graph-Theoretical View of Space Folding via the Motzkin–Straus Framework

In this paper we prove that the space folding measure is an analytic function of the Motzkin–Straus graph Lagrangian, thereby connecting folding to graph theory. This result turns a discrete folding statistic into a form that is easier to optimize.

Paper Workshop
Accepted at multiple International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) 2025 workshops

The Space Between: On Folding, Symmetries and Sampling

This paper extends the space folding measure from activation-pattern walks to walks through equivalence classes of inputs, and suggests using folding as a signal during learning. The workshop paper was later expanded into the AAAI paper on exploiting space folding.

Paper Workshop
NeurIPS 2024 Workshop on Symmetry and Geometry in Neural Representations

CantorNet: A Sandbox for Testing Geometrical and Topological Complexity Measures

CantorNet is a ReLU-network family inspired by the construction of the Cantor set. It analytically describes the same decision boundaries in two representations: one fractal-based and one obtained by merging polyhedral bodies. The former is provably the most compact representation in a Kolmogorov-style minimum-description-length sense, while the latter is the least compact.

Human-AI interaction

NeurIPS 2024 Workshop on System-2 Reasoning at Scale

The Turing Game

The Turing Game is a modern, gamified implementation of Turing's imitation game. It connects two human players with an LLM-powered chatbot in an online chat-based interaction, studying whether humans can distinguish peers from machines when the machine tries to blend in.

Mathematical physics

Banach Center Publications, 2017

Geometric features of Vessiot-Guldberg Lie algebras of conformal and Killing vector fields on \(\mathbb{R}^2\)

In this paper we classify finite-dimensional Vessiot-Guldberg Lie algebras of conformal and Killing vector fields on the plane. It was extended from my BSc thesis; the ideas therein can be used to connect preserved geometry in mathematical physics with modern symmetry-aware PDE learning and neural-operator modeling.

Statistics

MSc thesis, University of Warsaw / Inria Grenoble, 2018

Beyond the Generalized Pareto: A Fisher / Beta-Prime Tail Model for Peaks over Threshold

I studied a nested Fisher / beta-prime extension of the generalized Pareto exceedance model for finite-threshold extreme-value estimation. The goal was to improve tail and extreme-quantile estimation in the practical regime where thresholds are high, but samples remain finite.

Selected Applied Work

Selected applied and ongoing work outside individual paper entries.

Machine-learning surrogates for rotary-kiln calcination

At SCCH I work on machine-learning surrogates for calcination processes of limestone and magnesium carbonate in rotary kilns. The challenge is to approximate expensive physical simulations (CFD-based) with neural operator-based approaches. This simulation work is carried out within the PRIM-ROCK project.

Mechanistic interpretability of activation regions

I work on questions around the semanticity of activation regions in LLMs, and the geometry of CoT. The common theme is whether the information contained in the activation space can be used to mechanistically interpret model behavior.
